The 30-day plan

Simple. Structured.
It Actually Works.

Four weeks. Each one pushes you a little further without burning you out or making you dread tomorrow.

01
Week 1
Walk First

Interval walks with short running bursts. 15–20 min per session. Your body learns what running feels like — without the pain.

02
Week 2
Find A Rhythm

Run-walk-run. Your lungs open up. Your legs find a pace. Confidence builds faster than you think it will.

03
Week 3
Keep Going

Sustained running stretches. No more counting seconds — you just run. And it starts to actually feel good.

04
Week 4
Own The Run

Your first 5K attempt. You've done the hard part. Show up, run it, and earn the badge. It's waiting for you.

Before you ask

The Questions
You're Already Thinking.

I'm really unfit. Is this actually for me?

Yes — literally. Week 1 starts with walking. You don't run a single step until your body is ready. The program meets you exactly where you are.

I've tried to start running before and quit. Why now?

Because most programs assume motivation. We build structure and community instead — the two things that actually keep people going past Day 3.

How much time does it take each day?

15–30 minutes. Before work, during lunch, or after dinner. Most members run before 7 AM and are done in time for chai.

Do I need to buy special shoes or gear?

Any trainers you already own are fine for Week 1. We'll tell you exactly what to get if and when you need it — nothing premature.

Are the group runs really free with membership?

Yes. Weekly Sunday runs in all 5 cities are included. Just show up — we handle the route, the coaching, and the post-run chai meetup.

What happens after 30 days?

Most members continue with the intermediate plan or prepare for an official race event. The community doesn't stop at Day 31.
